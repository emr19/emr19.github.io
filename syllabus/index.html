What do students need to do in EMR19?

Project Sets (30% of final grade, approximately 5 throughout the term)

Each project set will require the students to either use an existing data set, or acquire his or her own, in order to produce a table, graph, chart, illustration, web page, presentation or some combination thereof. Basic algebraic, geometric and/or statistical calculations will be needed in preparing the data for display. It is assumed that all students have access to spreadsheet software (e.g. Excel, Numbers, Google Docs), but software beyond spreadsheets (e.g. Igor) will also be provided for the Project Sets. Students are encouraged to discuss the Project Sets in groups, but each student must turn in only his or her own work — not collaborative solutions. Exemplary student Project Sets will be selected and posted by the Teaching Fellows on the course web site. All Project Sets will be due at the start of the lecture on the day they are due. Late project sets will not be accepted without prior authorization from a TF.

Section/Online Participation (20%)

The course will meet twice per week in a lecture setting, and once per week in a small group setting, typically known as “section.” The purpose of the sectionkmeetings will not be primarily for review, but instead to give students and TFs the opportunity to delve into more real-life examples of the graphical principles discussed in lecture. Students will be asked to contribute (via an online tool) sample graphics (from any source) to be discussed in section each week. Student contributions will seed a new database aimed at creating enough information upon which researchers can base an “empirical theory of graphics.” Some section meetings will also feature "debates" where students use quantitative graphics to argue opposing sides.

Midterm Exam (20%)

The midterm exam will ask students to critique and analyze data from sample graphs, tables, and charts presented to them for the first time on the exam. Sample exam questions will be on the web site.

Final Project & Presentation (15% for presentation + 15% for documentation)

Each student, in consultation with a TF, will select a research topic on or beforeMarch 20. As long as the topic lends itself well to graphical quantitative examination and display, it does not matter what the subject matter is. In the past, topics have ranged from Shakespeare concordances to poker odds to war deaths to sailing and navigation practices.

The projects will first be presented in a live 8-minute presentation by each student in section, and the best presentation(s) from each section will be repeated in the course's concluding lecture slot. Then, one week after the last meeting of EMR19, each student will submit documentation of their project. Details on allowable formats (which include a standard “paper,” movies, interactives or web sites) will be discussed in class and in section during April.
